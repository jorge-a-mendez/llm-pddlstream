Since rover `v14` could see all the objectives with just one move, I chose that rover for the image collection task. Initially, the plan was to calibrate the camera and take all images one after the other. However, a deeper analysis of the `take_image` action revealed that it *invalidates* the camera calibration. Hence, I was forced to recalibrate the camera *before* each `take_image` action. This led to a revised plan for `v14` in which the calibrate/take_image cycle was iterated for each objective. The analysis actions were taken by `v12`, that moved to take the samples.
           
           The final, combined plan involves two rovers. Rover `v14` is responsible for capturing and sending images, following a series of calibration, image capture, and communication actions. It takes four images, moves to a communication config and sends all images. Rover `v12` navigates to the rock and soil samples, collects and sends analysis and comes back to its initial config. Crucially, I've incorporated return actions for each rover to ensure that the final state aligns perfectly with the goal's requirements, specifically ensuring that both rovers are back in their initial configurations at the plan's conclusion. The plan I have developed meets all the requirements from `new-axiom@0()`, including the correct final `atconf` positions, the successful transfer of images and analysis, and the returning of store spaces to a `free` status.
